Mount Everest

Mount Everest is the tallest mountain in the world, with a peak that rises to an elevation of 29,029 feet (8,848 meters) above sea level. It is located in the Himalaya mountain range, on the border between Nepal and Tibet (which is part of China). The mountain is named after Sir George Everest, a British surveyor who was the first person to accurately measure its height. Mount Everest is a popular destination for climbers and has been the site of many expeditions over the years. Climbing the mountain is considered a significant achievement in the world of mountaineering, but it is also a dangerous and challenging endeavor that requires careful planning and preparation.

Mountain K2

K2 is the second-tallest mountain in the world, with a peak that rises to an elevation of 28,251 feet (8,611 meters) above sea level. It is located on the border between Pakistan and China, in the Karakoram mountain range. K2 is considered one of the most difficult mountains in the world to climb, with a high fatality rate among climbers. It was first successfully climbed in 1954 by an Italian expedition. Despite its reputation as a challenging and dangerous mountain, K2 remains a popular destination for climbers and adventurers.

Mountain Kangchenjunga

Kangchenjunga is the third-highest mountain in the world, with a peak that rises to an elevation of 28,169 feet (8,586 meters) above sea level. It is located on the border between Nepal and India, in the Himalaya mountain range. The mountain is considered sacred by many local peoples, and climbing is prohibited on the peak itself. However, the surrounding area is popular among trekkers and climbers, and several other peaks in the area offer challenging climbs. Kangchenjunga was first successfully climbed in 1955 by a British expedition.

Mountain Lhotse

It is located on the border between Nepal and Tibet (which is part of China), and is connected to Mount Everest by a ridge called the South Col. Lhotse is a popular destination for climbers, and is considered one of the more challenging and technically difficult climbs in the Himalaya mountain range. Mountain Makalu

Makalu is the fifth-highest mountain in the world, with a peak that rises to an elevation of 27,838 feet (8,485 meters) above sea level. It is located on the border between Nepal and Tibet (which is part of China), in the Mahalangur Himalaya mountain range.
